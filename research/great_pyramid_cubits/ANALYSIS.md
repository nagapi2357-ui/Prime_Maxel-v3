## 4. Mathematical Relationships

### 4.1 π Relationships ★★★

| Relationship | Value | Target | Error |
|-------------|-------|--------|-------|
| Perimeter / Height | 1760/280 = **44/7** = 6.28571... | 2π = 6.28319... | **0.040%** |
| Half-perimeter / Height | 880/280 = **22/7** | π = 3.14159... | **0.040%** |
| Base / Height | 440/280 = **11/7** | π/2 = 1.57080... | **0.040%** |

**This is the famous 22/7 approximation of π**, built into the design through the seked of 5½ palms. The ratio 14/11 (rise/run) gives 4 × base / height = 4 × (11/7) = 44/7 ≈ 2π.

**Classification: VERY CLOSE APPROXIMATION — likely inherent in the seked choice, possibly intentional.**

### 4.2 Φ (Golden Ratio) Relationships ★★★

| Relationship | Value | Target | Error |
|-------------|-------|--------|-------|
| Apothem / Half-base | 356.09/220 = **1.61859** | Φ = 1.61803 | **0.034%** |
| Height / Half-base | 280/220 = **14/11** = 1.27273 | √Φ = 1.27202 | **0.056%** |
| Face area / (Half-base)² | **1.61859** | Φ = 1.61803 | **0.034%** |

**Critical insight:** These are NOT independent of the π relationship. For any pyramid where perimeter/height ≈ 2π:

sec(θ) = apothem/half-base = √(1 + tan²θ) where tan θ = 14/11

sec(arctan(4/π)) = √(1 + 16/π²) ≈ 1.6189 ≈ Φ

**The π and Φ relationships are mathematically coupled.** Choosing a seked that approximates π automatically approximates Φ. They are ONE relationship, not two.

### 4.6 The "Cubit Constant": π − Φ² ★★

```
π − Φ²  = 3.14159265... − 2.61803399... = 0.52355866...
RC      = 0.5236 m

Match:    0.5236 vs 0.5236  (to 4 decimal places)
Difference: 0.041 mm
```

This is a **remarkable numerical coincidence**. The Royal Cubit (based on forearm length, standardized across Egyptian history) happens to equal π − Φ² to 4 decimal places.

Since Φ² = Φ + 1, this is equivalently: **RC ≈ π − Φ − 1**

**Classification: NUMERICAL COINCIDENCE.** The cubit was an anthropometric unit. There is no mechanism by which forearm length would encode π − Φ². However, the coincidence is striking enough to be worth noting.

### 4.7 Pythagorean Relationships

The King's Chamber: 20 × 10 floor gives diagonal 10√5.
- If height = 5√5: body diagonal = √(400 + 100 + 125) = √625 = **25** (Pythagorean!)
- The triple (20, 10, 5√5) with diagonal 25 is a valid 3D Pythagorean relationship

## 5. The π − Φ² Recursion

### Do π and Φ reappear in ratios between cubit dimensions?

**YES — but this is tautological.** Since the external dimensions encode π (via 22/7), any ratio derived from them will contain π-related values:

| Ratio | Value | Mathematical constant |
|-------|-------|--------------------|
| Base/Height | 11/7 ≈ π/2 | π/2 = 1.5708 |
| Height/Half-base | 14/11 ≈ √Φ | √Φ = 1.2720 |
| Apothem/Half-base | ≈ Φ | Φ = 1.6180 |
| Half-base/Edge | ≈ π/6 | π/6 = 0.5236 **(!)** |
| Height/Perimeter | ≈ 1/(2π) | 0.1592 |

**The ratio Half-base/Edge ≈ 0.5256 is close to π/6 = 0.5236 (0.38% error)** — and π/6 is itself close to the cubit constant π − Φ². So yes, the "recursion" appears, but it's a consequence of the single design choice (seked = 5½).

### Does the pyramid encode π/6 internally?

π/6 = 0.5236... ≈ π − Φ² ≈ RC in metres. The ratio half-base/edge approximates this, but with 0.38% error — larger than the 0.04% of the primary π relationship. This is likely a secondary coincidence rather than an intentional encoding.

## 8. The Deep Insight

The Great Pyramid's mathematical elegance flows from **one design choice**: the seked of 5½ palms, giving a rise:run ratio of **14:11**.

This single ratio simultaneously produces:
1. **π** — because 4 × 11/14 × 2 = 44/7 ≈ 2π
2. **Φ** — because sec(arctan(14/11)) ≈ Φ (this is a mathematical identity that holds for any angle where tan gives 4/π)
3. **Near-integer dimensions** — because 11 and 7 are small primes

The question of whether the Egyptians chose this seked *because* it encodes π, or whether they chose it for practical/aesthetic reasons and the π relationship is emergent, remains one of the most debated questions in Egyptology.

The Rhind Mathematical Papyrus (c. 1650 BC, copied from a c. 1850 BC original) shows the Egyptians used the approximation (16/9)² = 256/81 ≈ 3.1605 for the ratio of a circle's area to the square of its radius — suggesting they had a practical working knowledge of π without formalizing it. The 22/7 approximation embedded in the Great Pyramid is considerably more accurate than the Rhind Papyrus value.

## 9. The Perfect Number Connection

**1 Royal Cubit = 7 palms × 4 fingers = 28 fingers**

**28 is a perfect number** — it equals the sum of its proper divisors:
28 = 1 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 14

Only the second perfect number (after 6). They are extraordinarily rare — the next are 496 and 8128.

### Deep Connections

| Property | Value | Significance |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| 28 = 2² × 7 | Prime factorisation | 7 = palms per cubit |
| 28 = 2^(3−1) × (2³−1) | Euclid-Euler formula | Mersenne prime M₃ = 7 |
| 28 = T(7) = 1+2+3+4+5+6+7 | 7th triangular number | Sum of all parts = whole |
| Pyramid height = 280 = 10 × 28 | Design multiple | 10 perfect-number units |
| Perimeter = 1760 = 62.857 × 28 ≈ 20π × 28 | π recursion | π appears again through 28 |

### Pythagorean Significance

The Pythagoreans considered perfect numbers divine — they represent complete harmony, where the parts exactly reconstitute the whole. This is Wheeler's principle: the divisions of the One perfectly sum back to the One.

The choice of 28 fingers per cubit was almost certainly deliberate — the unit of measurement itself embodies "wholeness" before you even start building with it.
